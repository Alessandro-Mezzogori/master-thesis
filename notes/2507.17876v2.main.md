<3,3,2,Q>: 
    how much negative data is needed to have the same result as the correct positive data ? 
    i would assume multiple order of magnitude more.
    this may not pose a problem due to most data being negative or partially negative for a specific task but maybe has impact at the computation level ? 

    GEMINI:
        It heavily depends on the specific chemical property being targeted, and your assumption that it requires orders of magnitude more data is not always correct. The authors analyzed the "positive-to-negative data exchange rates" and found that it varies significantly.

        - For properties like the fraction of sp3C or logP, positive data is highly efficient: just 10 positive molecules can outperform a model trained on 1024 negative molecules.
        - However, for properties like TPSA, the opposite is true: 50 negative molecules yielded the same number of successful design clusters as 500 positive molecules. In certain low-data scenarios, negative data can actually hold higher instructional value than positive data.


<3,3,3,Q>:
    zero-host ligand design ( generating data from looking only at negative samples ) could it lead to a way of working in which what is specified is the "not wanted" or maybe find a way to frame a "wanted" in a list of "not wanted" ? 

    GEMINI:
        Yes, this is exactly the core mechanism and breakthrough of Molecular Task Arithmetic (MTA). MTA views model "forgetting via subtraction" as a generative opportunity. By explicitly defining and training on what is "not wanted" (negative data), the method calculates a task vector. Subtracting this negative vector steers the pretrained model in the exact opposite direction in its weight space, generating "wanted" (positive) molecules without the model ever seeing a positive example.

<4,4,3,Q>:
    - what is a poor-docking molecule vs a weell-docking molecule

    GEMINI:
        These terms refer to how strongly a molecule binds to a target protein, which the authors defined statistically based on a baseline distribution:

        - Well-docking molecules: Ligands that score in the lowest 5% of binding energies when docked against a target, indicating a highly favorable and strong structural interaction.
        - Poor-docking molecules: Ligands that score in the highest 5% of binding energies, indicating weak or no favorable interaction with the target protein. Both classes are extremely scarce,    constituting less than 5% of the total pretraining set.

<4,5,1,Q>:
    is there a way that the average validity could be boosted,  while still using MTA ? 

    GEMINI:
        Yes. The paper outlines both practical heuristics and theoretical directions to mitigate the drop in validity (which occurs because pushing the model away from its pretraining weights disrupts its syntax generation capabilities).

        - Tuning the scaling factor (λ): Validity strongly correlates with how far the model is pushed in the weight space (λ). By keeping λ between 0.30 and 0.50, you achieve a peak success rate before generative validity collapses.
        - Future Architectural changes: The authors propose that future work using "selective task arithmetic" (updating only specific, critical weights instead of all weights) or alternative molecule representations could mitigate this validity drop.

<7,5,1,Q>
    cosa vuol dire out-of-distribution generalization ?

    GEMINI:
        In this context, OOD generalization refers to the model's ability to design molecules possessing property values that fall completely outside the numerical limits or bounds of the dataset it was pretrained on. It means the model can successfully extrapolate into entirely unexplored, "dark" regions of the chemical space where it has seen absolutely zero prior examples.

<7,5,2,Q>: 
    What does zero-shot dual-objective de novo design mean?

    GEMINI:
        This describes an advanced generative task combining three distinct difficulties:
        
        - De novo design: Generating completely novel chemical structures from scratch.
        - Dual-objective: The generated molecules must simultaneously satisfy two independent constraints (e.g., high lipophilicity and low TPSA).
        - Zero-shot: The model achieves this without ever being trained on, or provided with, any positive examples that possess those target properties. The vectors are simply calculated from negative data independently, summed, and subtracted.
